**The Problem**
Most people take notes that are messy, disorganized, and hard to review. This wastes time and reduces learning efficiency.

**The Exercise**
Today, use the **Cornell Note-Taking System** to organize your notes:
1. Divide your page into three sections: **Cues, Notes, Summary**.
2. Write key points in the **Notes** section.
3. Summarize the main idea in the **Summary** section.

**Why It Matters**
Effective note-taking improves memory retention and makes studying easier. It’s a skill that **AI can’t replicate**—it requires human organization and critical thinking.

**Counter-AI Connection**
AI tools like Otter.ai can transcribe lectures, but **only you can organize and understand** the information. Train your brain to process and retain knowledge.
