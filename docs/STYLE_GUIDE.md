# Style Guide for Daily Upgrade Content Engine

This style guide ensures consistency across all content produced for the **Daily Upgrade Content Engine**. Follow these guidelines for writing, formatting, and designing content.

## Writing Style

### Tone and Voice
- **Tone:** Inspirational, actionable, and conversational.
- **Voice:** Empathetic, intelligent, and encouraging.
- **Audience:** Lifelong learners, professionals, and anyone interested in cognitive independence.

### Grammar and Mechanics
- Use **active voice** and **strong verbs**. Avoid passive constructions.
- Keep sentences and paragraphs short for readability.
- Use **bullet points** and **subheadings** to break up text.
- Avoid jargon unless explained.

### Formatting
- Use **Markdown** for all text-based content.
- Follow the templates provided in the `content/templates` directory.
- Use **bold** for emphasis and *italics* for quotes or special terms.

## Visual Style

### Images
- Use high-quality, relevant images.
- Optimize images for web (compress if necessary).
- Follow the **image prompts** provided in each post for consistency.

### Infographics
- Use the **brand color palette** (see below).
- Keep designs clean and easy to understand.
- Include the **Counter-AI Movement logo** or tagline where appropriate.

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** `#2c3e50` (Dark Blue)
- **Secondary:** `#3498db` (Bright Blue)
- **Accent:** `#e74c3c` (Red)
- **Background:** `#f9f9f9` (Light Gray)

## Content Structure

### Blog Posts
- **Title:** Clear, actionable, and aligned with the **Counter-AI Movement**. Example: "Brain Upgrade #1: The Dangers of AI Dependency"
- **Introduction:** Hook the reader with a compelling problem or question.
- **Body:** Break into sections with clear subheadings.
- **Conclusion:** End with a **call to action** (CTA) and **bonus tool/resource**.

### Support Content
- **YouTube/TikTok/Reels:** Scripts should be engaging, concise, and include a **CTA** to the blog post.
- **Infographics:** Follow the outline provided in the templates. Use visuals to simplify complex ideas.

## Naming Conventions
- **Files:** Use `yyyy-mm-dd-title-format.ext` (e.g., `2026-01-01-dangers-of-ai-dependency.md`).
- **Folders:** Use lowercase, hyphen-separated names (e.g., `brain-upgrades`).
